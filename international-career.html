Early Call-Up and Debut (Age 21)

Paulo Dybala’s journey with the Argentina national team began at the age of 21, when he received his first senior call-up in September 2015.
Although eligible to play for Italy or Poland through his ancestry, Dybala made it clear that he identified fully as Argentinian and would only represent Argentina internationally.
He made his official senior debut on October 13, 2015, coming on as a substitute in a World Cup qualifier against Paraguay.
At the time, he was emerging as one of Europe’s brightest young forwards, starring for Juventus in Serie A.

Competition for Spots and 2018 World Cup (Age 24)

Despite his immense talent, Dybala struggled to establish himself as a regular starter in the national team during his early years.
This was partly due to the depth of Argentina’s attacking talent and the difficulty coaches had in playing both Dybala and Lionel Messi in the same lineup.
He was selected for the 2018 FIFA World Cup in Russia at age 24, but made just one appearance, coming off the bench during the group stage against Croatia.
Argentina exited the tournament in the Round of 16 after a loss to France.

Copa América 2019 – First Competitive Goal (Age 25)

Dybala's role became more prominent at the 2019 Copa América in Brazil.
At 25 years old, he featured more regularly and scored his first competitive goal for Argentina in the third-place playoff against Chile, helping secure a 2–1 win.
This was a breakthrough moment for him on the international stage, showing he could contribute in high-pressure matches.

Finalissima and Other Contributions (Age 26-27)

In 2022, at age 26, Dybala played a key role in Argentina's victory in the CONMEBOL–UEFA Cup of Champions (Finalissima) against Italy.
He scored the opening goal in a 3-0 win, showcasing his ability to perform in crucial matches.
Throughout this period, Dybala continued to be a valuable squad member, often coming off the bench to provide creativity and attacking options.
Over his career, he has also scored in international friendlies, including a goal against Mexico in 2018.

World Cup Glory – Qatar 2022 (Age 29)

The pinnacle of Dybala's international career came at the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar, where he was part of the Argentina squad that won the tournament.
At 29 years old, Dybala played a significant role throughout the competition, often coming off the bench to make an impact.
He scored a crucial goal in the quarterfinal against the Netherlands, helping Argentina advance to the semifinals.
In the final against France, Dybala came on as a substitute and contributed to Argentina's victory in the penalty shootout, securing their third World Cup title.
Dybala earned his place in the nation’s football history as part of a World Cup–winning team.

Legacy and Role in the National Team (Age 30 and Beyond)

While Paulo Dybala has never been the main star of the Argentina national team, largely due to the presence of Lionel Messi and a crowded attacking lineup, his career is a testament to patience, professionalism, and clutch performances.
Whether stepping up in the World Cup final shootout or scoring in key tournaments, Dybala has proven to be a valuable part of Argentina's golden era — contributing to victories in the Copa América, Finalissima, and World Cup.
