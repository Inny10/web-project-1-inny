Early Call-Up and Debut (Age 21)

Paulo Dybala’s journey with the Argentina national team began at the age of 21, when he received his first senior call-up in September 2015.
Although eligible to play for Italy or Poland through his ancestry, Dybala made it clear that he identified fully as Argentinian and would only represent Argentina internationally.
He made his official senior debut on October 13, 2015, coming on as a substitute in a World Cup qualifier against Paraguay.
At the time, he was emerging as one of Europe’s brightest young forwards, starring for Juventus in Serie A.

Competition for Spots and 2018 World Cup (Age 24)

Despite his immense talent, Dybala struggled to establish himself as a regular starter in the national team during his early years.
This was partly due to the depth of Argentina’s attacking talent and the difficulty coaches had in playing both Dybala and Lionel Messi in the same lineup.
He was selected for the 2018 FIFA World Cup in Russia at age 24, but made just one appearance, coming off the bench during the group stage against Croatia.
Argentina exited the tournament in the Round of 16 after a loss to France.

Copa América 2019 – First Competitive Goal (Age 25)

Dybala's role became more prominent at the 2019 Copa América in Brazil.
At 25 years old, he featured more regularly and scored his first competitive goal for Argentina in the third-place playoff against Chile, helping secure a 2–1 win.
This was a breakthrough moment for him on the international stage, showing he could contribute in high-pressure matches.
